Being a leader in my field (Principal Engineer)

In the section below, please find my analysis of how I can concretely practice the skills of Managing Conflict & Dealing with Difficult People to become an effective Principal Engineer.

What does it mean to be a Principal Engineer? What will it take to get me there? What skills do I already possess that will help me become the leader?
Since the technical leader of a principal engineer is tasked with some of the highest leverage technical decisions, conflict is unavoidable. The stakes are increased beyond that which a staff-level engineer is faced with. The impact of this leader making a wrong decision can be detrimental to the entire company. It is this leader's responsibility though to lead through conflict and find ways to deal with the difficult people around us.
I personally still struggle with the real negative aspects of conflict at work. I am not alone though; this will likely always be a component of conflict for me but finding healthy ways to cope with the stress and proceed is the goal of being an effective Principal Engineer.
I tend to control my own emotions well and others won't necessarily observe if I'm having difficulty in a conflict. This is an important skill to have and help keep the tone light when leading through conflict.

Artifact 4 Emotional Intelligence Case Study 3 and Discussion

Concept & Illustrating Artifacts

During Module 4 we had an assignment titled “Emotional Intelligence Case Studies”. Each member of our group was tasked with considering a few scenarios where there was conflict, and we played the role of the leader in an attempt to resolve the conflict. I won't share the discussion here since this website is public, but I would recommend those with access to the canvas discussion to review it. The case study I choose to analyze was a conflict between CiCi and Daisy who work in our customer service department. There is a training that CiCi prepares, but Daisy is not able to follow the format of the training. The situation escalates with some gossiping and is articulated by Daisy leaving work abruptly citing the stress of the incident. The objective of the case study is to find a way to lead CiCi and Daisy to reconcile their differences.

In our group discussion it was interesting discussing with Erin and Luis. Erin and I both addressed this prompt but in different ways. Luis was interested enough in our responses to read this prompt in addition to the prompt he had responded to!

Reflection on how this knowledge and skills will help you become a good leader

Given the facts of the case study, I believe my approach of separating the case study into two issues is productive. There was a substantive issue of the training which is not as effective as the group wanted. There was also a culture issue which is more concerning to me of the gossiping and effect on a team member to the point of leaving the workplace due to stress.

I really appreciated Erin's response and discussion too. I did not even consider that the task that was assigned to CiCi may not yet have been within her grasp, and this may have exacerbated the already weak relationship between her and Daisy. I think through this discussion I have grown as a leader because my perspective is now broader regarding conflict in the workplace.

Taking my leadership in this area to the next level will require more successful examples like we consider in the case study. It would be helpful to have more impactful substantive issues though. Daisy leaving work due to a conflict is already a very extreme culture issue to respond to.

Plan for getting there

I am not in a position to take the role of the leader resolving an issue between two reports. However, there are many conflicts that don't require that formal working relationship. I can practice these techniques in my current role especially on high stakes substantive conflicts. The next time I am facing a substantive conflict I can prepare my notes and gather data and approach the conflict directly and seek change.
