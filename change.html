Being a leader in my field (Principal Engineer)

In the section below, please find my analysis of how I can concretely practice the skills of Influencing Change & Revisiting Leverage to become an effective Principal Engineer.

What does it mean to be a Principal Engineer? What will it take to get me there? What skills do I already possess that will help me become the leader?

When I become a principal engineer, an individual contributor who is a technical leader. My time will be limited and valuable. As one person I will not have end-to-end ownership of projects, I will not be at every early discussion of a project. However, I will be brought into situations where change is desperately needed. When I do become involved in a situation, I need to quickly identify what the issue is and produce a recommendation for how to proceed. As a leader it is my responsibility to triage issues and identify if they need more or less attention and involvement from other leaders.

There is an element of showmanship required to get the attention of a larger group of individuals. As a principal engineer, when I consider how change models like John Kotter’s eight step process apply to the roll I think of a few activities. I need to create a sense of urgency to unfreeze the organization. I can use data, statistics, and technical demos to show others a vision for the future. I need to facilitate the formation of a guiding coalition and get the organization into the movement phase where change happens. Finally, I need to refreeze the norm by showcasing the accomplishment and setting up systems to prevent a regression to the old way of working.

Part of my issue is in my current role I would not likely be granted the authority to do some of these tasks. I need to take the opportunities when they present themselves. Even partial involvement such as being part of the guiding coalition is a good experience to learn concretely how organizations handle change beyond theory.
I have had a few projects where I was given control and have been successful in leading change. A point of improvement for me though is building rapport amongst the coalition instead of taking the position that I am assigning action items, instead it should be driven by the coalition’s desire to enable the change.

Reflection on how this knowledge and skills will help you become a good leader

My initial reaction to this issue is that I would inform my mentor about it, and they would act and solve the problem for me. Instead of doing that he led me through a discussion of problem solving, we brainstormed solutions to the problem and that is how I arrived at the action item of meeting with the stakeholders.
